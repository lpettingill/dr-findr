About Lindsay Pettingill

I'm finishing up a PhD in Government at Georgetown; my dissertation is on political behavior in diverse societies. I am a 2002 Bowdoin grad. Go U Bears!

My work is diverse. It is united by a desire to use data to understand the world around us. I leverage unique sources of data– millions of phone calls to city service lines, pirate attacks, bomb damage to cities in World War II, for example, alongside demographic and economic data– to understand who we are and how we act.

I also like to surf, boulder, and bike. One of the coolest trips I've taken was a solo bike tour across the islands of Southern Denmark. I made my way from Rostock to Flensburg via Denmark on various ferries and my single speed (The Green Monster), armed with a Danish book on free places to camp, Google Translate, and lots of dried apricots. I cursed at the wind.
